Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing & Language Arts Integration

In real-life context, English language is important. The need to use integrated-skills instruction relates to the fact that in real-life communication, language is used in an interrelated manner. Learners never use listening, speaking, reading, or writing in isolation, but rather combine the skills to comprehend and convey meaning. Thus, skills inclusion in the lesson will assist pupils to learn English in a more meaningful and effective way. It also enables students to consolidate on what they have studied because one competence facilitates another. To illustrate, listening tasks can be input that pupils will utilize in speaking or writing. This renders the process of learning more interesting, practical and easier to remember by the pupils.

It is a balance between listening, speaking, reading, writing and language arts that is incorporated in this set of lesson plans. The lesson of listening is based on the comprehension of everyday life based on audio and is combined with speaking and vocabulary, during which the pupils answer questions and create simple sentences. The speech lesson combines listening and language arts by using songs and interactive lessons, where the pupils are able to rehearse prepositions in an effective and entertaining setting. The reading lesson combines writing and vocabulary, in which the pupils read simple texts and combine the vocabulary to form sentences. The writing lesson is a combination of reading and grammar whereby emphasis is laid on sentence construction through proper structures. Moreover, the lesson in the field of language arts combines all four skills with the help of the creative activities (songs, role-play, and games).

The learners targeted are primary school students whose level of proficiency varies. The lessons will be easy, interactive, and facilitating, and they will involve visuals, digital tools, and guided activities to make the pupils learn and engage. Pupils know the context of the lessons, e.g. their daily routines and classroom scenarios, so they can make the learning relevant to their own experiences and this will promote active learning.

Lesson 1: Listening

(Speaking + Vocabulary) - Daily Routines

  • Identify 3 daily routines from the audio correctly when asked question by their peers.
  • Match 3 routines to the correct room in the house into the projected slide.
  • Answer simple questions orally (e.g., “Where?”).
  • Produce at least 1 simple sentence/phrase about daily routine.
  • Pre Lesson: Activate prior knowledge. Pupils will be displayed a digital house diagram (e.g., via Canva) showing different rooms.
  • Stage I: Vocabulary. Pupils will be presented with Canva tools that have a 2d cross section of a house. Then, the students will be handed out a set of flashcards.
  • Stage II: Listening (Integrate with speaking). The pupils will be divided into groups to play competitive games.
  • Stage III: Speaking Activity. Pupils will be given a worksheet of “Guess Where Am I” and divided into pairs.
  • Post Lesson: Closure. Pupils will look again at Canva and answer teacher questions.

Lesson 2: Speaking

(Listening + Language Arts) - Prepositions

  • Identify 3 prepositions of place correctly through actions.
  • Drag and put 3 items correctly according to the preposition based on audio instructions.
  • Ask and answer 2 simple questions using correct prepositions with a dialog helper.
  • Pre Lesson: Pupils observe a durian plushie and guess what it is. The teacher introduces prepositions.
  • Stage I: Presentation. Pupils listen to a preposition song and act out positions.
  • Stage II: Practice (Listening Game). Small groups play a prepositions game online based on audio instructions.
  • Stage III: Production (Speaking). Pupils work in pairs with dialog strips which vary by proficiency.
  • Post Lesson: Exit Ticket. 3 pupils volunteer to come in front and practice asking/answering.

Lesson 3: Reading

(Writing + Vocabulary) - CVC Words

  • Blend phonemes to form 3 CVC words correctly using a CVC word-building slider worksheet.
  • Read and match 3 CVC words correctly to the correct pictures based on the worksheet.
  • Write at least 3 CVC words correctly based on the words formed during the activity.
  • Pre Lesson: Activate prior phonics knowledge. Pupils recap the sound of alphabets sound.
  • Stage I: Introduction to CVC blending. Pupils identify and articulate individual phonemes.
  • Stage II: Guided construction and reading. Pupils construct CVC words using the slider worksheet.
  • Stage III: Independent word construction. Pupils in small groups receive a “fries box” containing incomplete CVC word parts.
  • Post Lesson: Reflection. Pupils recall and verbalise CVC words constructed during the lesson.

Lesson 4: Writing

(Reading + Grammar) - Daily Activities

  • Identify correct present continuous sentences from given options.
  • Complete sentences using correct verb-ing forms.
  • Write at least 3 simple sentences using present continuous tense accurately.
  • Pre Lesson: Activate prior knowledge. Pupils observe several action pictures displayed.
  • Stage I: Pupils focus on the web slide. Pupils observe sentence models.
  • Stage II: Fish(ING) Action! Pupils form group to compete in fishing game containing a task.
  • Stage III: Pupils receive a worksheet with pictures to write 3-5 sentences independently.
  • Post Lesson: Exit Ticket. A sentence is written on the whiteboard, pupils volunteer to correct the sentence.

Lesson 5: Language Arts

(Vocabulary & Grammar) - My Feelings

  • Identify 3 feelings from the song correctly.
  • Respond imaginatively through speaking, acting, and writing during the board game activity.
  • Pre Lesson: Introduction to feelings vocabulary. Pupils observe a picture of an angry man.
  • Stage I: Presentation. The Feelings Song. Pupils identify the feelings in the song by showing the designated emotion card based on the lyrics.
  • Stage II: Practice. (Memory Retention Through TPR) Face Fun! and Face Switch!.
  • Stage III: Production (All skills Integration). Feeling Valley. Pupils form 4 groups and take turns to roll the dice.
  • Post Lesson: Exit Ticket. Pupils observe the teacher’s facial expressions and respond to questions.
